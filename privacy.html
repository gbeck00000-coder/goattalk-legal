Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Goat Talk Studio (the "Application") handles information when used to upload short-form vertical video content to TikTok.

1. Information We Access

When you authorize the Application to access your TikTok account, we request the following scopes from TikTok's Login Kit:

  • user.info.basic — your TikTok user identifier and basic profile fields, used solely to identify which account the Application is uploading to.
  • video.upload — permission to upload a video file to your TikTok account inbox/drafts. The Application uploads videos that you have generated locally; you decide whether to publish each draft from inside the TikTok app.

2. What We Do Not Collect

The Application does not collect, store, sell, share, or transmit any of the following:

  • Your TikTok password.
  • Your TikTok followers, following list, comments, messages, or other account content.
  • Your videos beyond the single file you choose to upload.
  • Analytics, telemetry, or usage data about you or your device.

3. How We Store Authorization Tokens

OAuth access and refresh tokens issued by TikTok during authorization are stored locally on the operator's computer in a file named .tiktok_tokens.json. They are not transmitted to any third-party server. They are used solely to call TikTok's official API endpoints on behalf of the authorizing account.

4. Third Parties

The Application does not share access tokens, account information, or video content with any third party. The Application makes API calls only to TikTok's official endpoints at open.tiktokapis.com.

5. Revoking Access

You can revoke the Application's access at any time by visiting your TikTok account settings → "Manage app permissions" and removing the Application. After revocation, any stored tokens become invalid.

6. Children

The Application is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly process information from children.

7. Contact

8. Changes

If this Privacy Policy is updated, the "Last updated" date at the top of the page will be revised.
